Satoru Iwata

Widely regarded as someone who helped make video games liked by a wider audience by focusing on making entertaining games rather than advanced hardware.

Here's a timeline of Iwata's life:

  • 1959 - born on December 6th in Sapporo, Hokkaido, Japan
  • 1974 - purchased an HP-65, the first programmable calculator
  • 1975 - entered Hokkaido Sapporo South High School and started developing own games
  • 1978 - admitted to Tokyo Institute of Technology
  • 1980 - while attending University, he worked for HAL Laboratory as a part-time programmer
  • 1982 - he joined the company in a full-time capacity after graduating
  • 1983 - he became the company's coordinator of software production
  • 1993 - promoted to president of HAL
  • 1999 - assisted in developement of Pokémon Gold and Silver
  • 2000 - joined Nintendo
  • 2002 - becomes Nintendo fourth president
  • 2003 - keynote speech at the Tokyo Game Show
  • 2004 - major reorganization of Nintendo
  • 2005 - Brain Age: Train Your Brain in Minutes a Day! game on Nintendo DS
  • 2010 - start of Nintendo's finances downward turn
  • 2011 - he halved his salary as apologies for poor sales
  • 2012 - release of the Wii U
  • 2013 - he took additional role of Nintendo of America's CEO
  • 2014 - he halved his salary as apologies for poor sales
  • 2015 - died on July 11 in Kyoto University Hospital, Kyoto, Japan
"Throughout the history of game development, the game control mechanism has become more and more sophisticated, ... Perhaps those who have quit gaming or who have never played games look at the game controller and think it's too difficult to play, even before they dare to touch it." — Satoru Iwata
